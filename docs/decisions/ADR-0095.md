# ADR-0095 — RIST (Reliable Internet Stream Transport) input + output: tiered transport seam

**Status:** Proposed (2026-06-13)
**Area:** streaming/timing (T) — contribution/distribution transport (implementation)
**Drivers:** the RIST-as-a-first-class-transport requirement (operator backlog item 2026-06-13 —
RIST is the open VSF standard peer of SRT, required as an ingest **and** egress protocol) and the
LGPL-clean-default + open-standards mandate (CLAUDE.md §1/§7,
[conventions §7](../architecture/conventions.md)).
**Research:** [rist-transport](../research/rist-transport.md).
**Related:** [ADR-0039](ADR-0039.md) (SRT tiered transport seam — the **sibling pattern this ADR
deliberately mirrors**), [ADR-0028](ADR-0028.md) (own-the-FFI-binding pattern — the Tier-2 model),
[ADR-0031](ADR-0031.md) (our-built LGPL-clean FFmpeg — must add `--enable-librist`),
[ADR-0040](ADR-0040.md) (multicast transport — typed-config-not-raw-URL precedent),
[ADR-0034](ADR-0034.md) (decoupled routing / `StreamInventory`),
[ADR-0035](ADR-0035.md) (capability detection + `HealthWarning` catalog — the RIST-stats health
surface), [ADR-0042](ADR-0042.md) (IPv6-first), [ADR-T003](ADR-T003.md) (unified timing — never
float, re-stamp from tick), [ADR-R001](ADR-R001.md)/[ADR-R003](ADR-R003.md) (continuous-output
guarantee + supervised reconnect). **RIST here = the network transport (VSF TR-06 / librist), not
subtitles.**

> **ADR numbering.** RIST is an IO/Transport sibling of SRT (ADR-0039), multicast (ADR-0040), and
> SAP (ADR-0041), all of which took **numeric** ADRs (the `T`-prefix is reserved for pure
> timing-model decisions; transport-*implementation* decisions use the numeric series and carry the
> "streaming/timing (T)" area label, exactly as ADR-0039 does). This ADR sits at **0095** rather
> than the next-after-its-siblings 0060: the contiguous block **0060–0094** was reserved by the
> 2026-06-13 feature-intake batch (16 briefs across 38 intake requests, e.g.
> observability-logging = 0060), authored in parallel on `docs/feature-intake-2026-06-13`. RIST is
> **not** a duplicate of any of those — only the number collided — so this ADR was renumbered out of
> their reserved range to **0095**, the next free number past their block (gaps in the numeric series
> are fine and intentional here). 0095 still keeps RIST in the numeric (not `T`-prefix) series
> adjacent to its transport siblings — SRT (ADR-0039), multicast (ADR-0040), SAP (ADR-0041) — and
> matches the demonstrated precedent; the 0060–0094 gap simply records the feature-intake reservation
> so a future reader understands why RIST is not at 0060.

## Context

Multiview names SRT as a first-class ingest+egress transport (CLAUDE.md §1) and ships it through a
**tiered seam** (ADR-0039): FFmpeg's `srt://` caller for the license-clean baseline, owned Rust/FFI
for what FFmpeg structurally cannot do, with the libsrt-bonding FFI leaf deferred. **RIST is the
open-standard sibling of SRT** — the VSF TR-06 family: RTP/UDP media with **NACK-based ARQ
retransmission** sized to an RTT-derived receiver buffer. Broadcasters routinely require *both*
because upstream/downstream partners standardize on one or the other; supporting RIST closes a
"can't take your feed / can't deliver to your endpoint" gap.

The as-built seam already fits RIST with almost no new shape (verified by reading the source):
network `SourceKind` variants (`Rtsp`/`Hls`/`Ts`/`Srt`/`Rtmp`, `schema.rs`) carry a `url` and flow
through one libav `Demuxer` (`demux.rs`); egress `Output::Srt`/`Rtmp` → `PushProtocol`
(`sink.rs`, `muxer_name`=`mpegts` for SRT/UDP) → `PacketMuxSink::push`, fanned by `run_push_output`
(`pipeline.rs`) as encode-once-mux-many (inv #7). A `rist://` URL is opened by libav identically to
`srt://` when FFmpeg is built `--enable-librist`.

**Licensing is favourable and verified.** librist (the reference C library, VideoLAN/SipRadius) is
**BSD-2-Clause** — permissive, LGPL-compatible. FFmpeg's `--enable-librist` forces neither
`--enable-gpl` nor `--enable-nonfree`, so the default LGPL-clean build (ADR-0031) stays clean. RIST
belongs in the **permissive/LGPL-clean** dependency tier alongside libsrt (MPL-2.0), **not** the
license-escalating tier (`gpl-codecs` / proprietary NDI).

**The decisive constraint is what FFmpeg's `librist` protocol does NOT expose** (verified against
`libavformat/librist.c`):
- It calls **`rist_peer_create()` exactly once** against one parsed URI → **no bonding /
  load-sharing / seamless multi-link switching** (which is RIST's headline selling point and is a
  **Simple-Profile** capability — *not* Main-only). librist *does* support bonding via multiple
  `rist_peer_create` calls, but only through direct FFI.
- It registers **no `rist_stats` callback and no stats AVOption** → **no link statistics**
  (retransmit/loss/RTT/bandwidth) for the `HealthWarning` surface.
- It exposes **only PSK-AES encryption** (`secret`+`encryption` 128/256), not DTLS or EAP-SRP (TR-06-3).
- It *does* expose `rist_profile` (simple/main/advanced, default main), `buffer_size` (ms ARQ
  window), `pkt_size` (1316), `fifo_size`, `overrun_nonfatal`, `log_level` — sufficient for
  single-link Simple/Main + PSK.

So RIST is a *tiering* problem identical to SRT, not a greenfield build: a thin, license-clean
single-link baseline through FFmpeg, plus owned FFI for the two things FFmpeg can't do (stats,
bonding), the latter deferred.

## Decision

1. **Tiered transport seam (mirrors ADR-0039).** Implement RIST as input **and** output behind a
   single off-by-default **`rist`** feature, in tiers:
   - **Tier-0 — FFmpeg `rist://` protocol (ship first, the baseline).** Route RIST through libav's
     `librist` protocol: ingest via the existing `Demuxer` (a `SourceKind::Rist` URL), egress via a
     new `PushProtocol::Rist` (`muxer_name` = `mpegts`) consuming the **same** encoded packets as
     every other push sink (inv #7). Covers **single-link Simple + Main Profile with pre-shared-key
     AES-128/256** and the RTT/buffer ARQ window. No new FFI; minimal diff; LGPL-clean.
   - **Tier-1 — link statistics (own what FFmpeg can't).** A thin `multiview-rist-sys` FFI leaf
     (ADR-0028 pattern) wiring `rist_stats_callback_set` → telemetry + the `HealthWarning` catalog
     (ADR-0035). Off the data plane, watch/broadcast drop-oldest (inv #10).
   - **Tier-2 — bonding / load-sharing / seamless switching (DEFER, only-if-required).** Multiple
     `rist_peer_create` into one librist ctx, plus DTLS/EAP-SRP, on the same FFI leaf. Built **only**
     when a concrete multi-ISP requirement lands — exactly as SRT defers its libsrt-bonding leaf.
2. **Typed config, not raw-URL query-soup (mirrors ADR-0040).** Add
   `SourceKind::Rist { url, rist: Option<RistOptions> }` and
   `Output::Rist { id, url, codec, gpu_pin, audio }` with a typed `RistOptions`
   (`profile: RistProfile`, `buffer_ms`, `pkt_size`, `encryption: RistEncryption`, `bonding:
   Vec<RistPeer>`). `RistOptions` *validates* and *lowers* to the `rist://…?…` AVIO URL (Tier-0) and
   to a librist peer list (Tier-2). Internally-tagged serde, `#[non_exhaustive]`, `skip_serializing_if`.
3. **Secrets are reference-only.** The PSK passphrase is a `secret_ref` (the existing `SourceAuth`
   secret-manager pattern), resolved at run time, **never** stored in config or logs. Provide
   `to_url` / `to_url_redacted` (the SRT precedent) so the plaintext secret only ever exists in the
   in-memory AVIO URL.
4. **Honest capability reporting.** A non-empty `bonding` list configured against a build that has
   only Tier-0 compiled is **rejected at validate time with a clear "bonding requires the `rist`
   direct-FFI build" error** — never silently single-linked. (No parked stub; the explicit error is
   the complete, honest behaviour for that build.)
5. **License tier.** `rist` joins the permissive/LGPL-clean tier in conventions §4/§7
   (BSD-2-Clause librist; `--enable-librist` on our own-built FFmpeg, ADR-0031). `deny.toml` must
   allow BSD-2-Clause (confirm at implementation; almost certainly already allowed).
6. **IPv6-first** (ADR-0042): RIST listen defaults to dual-stack `rist://[::]:port`, loopback
   `[::1]`, IPv6 literals bracketed in the lowered URL; examples lead IPv6.
7. **Invariants are non-negotiable.** RIST ingest is **sampled** behind the per-tile last-good store
   (its ARQ buffer is an input jitter buffer, **never** the output clock — inv #1/#2/#3); RIST egress
   is a mux-many fan-out off a **bounded drop-oldest** queue, the engine never `.await`s the socket
   (inv #7/#10). Input PTS go through the standard normalize/rebase pipeline and output PTS are
   re-stamped from the tick (inv #3). RIST loss/retransmit/reconnect is **expected** behaviour the
   tile state machine rides — bad-inputs-are-the-purpose — never log-spam or a stall.

## Consequences

- **Single-link RIST in/out ships fast, license-clean, with a minimal diff** that reuses the exact
  SRT-shaped demux/mux seam. This is the 90% contribution/distribution case.
- **Bonding — RIST's headline feature — is NOT in Tier-0.** It is genuinely unreachable through
  FFmpeg's protocol (single `rist_peer_create`) and requires the Tier-2 direct-FFI leaf. The schema
  carries the `bonding` field and rejects it honestly on a Tier-0-only build. **If the operator's
  priority is multi-ISP bonding from day one, Tier-2 must be built up front** — flagged for the
  operator; the default plan defers it.
- **Link statistics need Tier-1 (direct FFI).** Until Tier-1, RIST link health is invisible beyond
  libav's log; the chaos gate still proves recovery, but the operator has no retransmit/RTT readout.
  Tier-1 is therefore the first Option-B increment in the backlog.
  - **Tier-1 SHIPPED (RIST-5).** `multiview-rist-sys` runtime-loads librist (`dlopen`, never linked at
    build time, BSD-2-Clause), wires `rist_stats_callback_set` on an **owned** librist context, and
    decodes the `rist_stats` union (sender-peer / receiver-flow). The decoded sample lowers to the
    `multiview-telemetry` metric series (rtt/quality/bandwidth gauges + cumulative-as-gauge
    retransmit/lost/recovered/sent), a conflated `rist.link.stats` realtime event (rides `outputs`),
    and the `rist-link-loss` health warning. Behind the off-by-default `rist-stats` feature.
    **Honest boundary (the adversarial finding, resolved):** stats cannot be bolted onto FFmpeg's
    owned `rist://` socket, so the stats-bearing path is a **direct-librist sender** egress
    (`multiview-output::rist::RistSenderLink`) — leaf-sized, a sink consuming already-encoded packets
    (inv #7), replacing no shared transport. The direct-librist **receiver** with stats owns the
    receive+demux loop (a new `Source`) and is the Tier-2-shaped follow-up — **not** built; the
    decode + bridge already handle the receiver role. No stat is ever fabricated.
- The own-built FFmpeg (ADR-0031) must add `--enable-librist` to its LGPL-clean profile — a build
  recipe change, no license change.
- A **lossy-link chaos/soak gate** (netem/tc loss+jitter+blackout) becomes part of CI for RIST — the
  proof that the recovery actually works through our pipeline (inv #1/#2/#10). This is the most
  important RIST test.
- The WebUI/management surface gains a RIST source/output type; bonding bandwidth implications
  (replicate = N× uplink) must be surfaced so an operator does not accidentally multiply their uplink.

## Alternatives rejected

- **Direct librist FFI for everything, from day one (Option B only).** Rejected as the *starting*
  point: it is a large `unsafe` FFI surface and maintenance burden for a capability (bonding/stats)
  most single-link deployments never use, and it discards the zero-FFI, license-clean, fits-the-seam
  Tier-0 win. We adopt it **incrementally** (Tier-1 stats, then Tier-2 bonding only-if-required), not
  as the baseline — the exact SRT decision.
- **FFmpeg protocol forever (Option A only, no FFI seam).** Rejected: it structurally cannot do
  bonding or stats (verified), which are real RIST capabilities operators will eventually need.
  Pretending the FFmpeg path is the whole story would be dishonest capability reporting.
- **Raw opaque `rist://?…` URL in the config (no typed options).** Rejected per the ADR-0040
  precedent: query-soup URLs are fragile, un-validatable, un-redactable (secrets leak into logs), and
  hostile to the WebUI. Typed `RistOptions` that lower to the URL is the established pattern.
- **A `gpl-codecs`-style license-escalating gate for `rist`.** Rejected and unnecessary: librist is
  BSD-2-Clause, `--enable-librist` keeps FFmpeg LGPL — RIST is permissive/LGPL-clean, an off-by-default
  *native-dep* gate only, not a license-escalating one.
- **DTLS/EAP-SRP in Tier-0.** Rejected/scoped out: FFmpeg's protocol exposes only PSK-AES. Tier-0 ships
  PSK honestly; DTLS/EAP-SRP are Tier-1/2 direct-FFI features, not claimed before they are built.
