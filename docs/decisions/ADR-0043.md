# ADR-0043: Relicense Multiview from MIT OR Apache-2.0 to a source-available non-commercial license (dual-license)

- **Status:** Accepted
- **Area:** Core Engine — Licensing
- **Date:** 2026-06-08
- **Supersedes:** [ADR-0012](ADR-0012.md) (which was only ever *Proposed*)
- **Source advisory:** [docs/licensing/relicense-advisory.md](../licensing/relicense-advisory.md)

## Context

We are in the pre-public-release window: the repository has **never** been
published, so no permissive public copies are outstanding and there is no
downstream community with vested `MIT OR Apache-2.0` rights to a public version.
This is the one moment a relicense can be done cleanly — after even a single
public tag, anyone could fork that version and continue it under the original
permissive terms forever.

The product goal is **free for genuine personal/home/non-commercial use, paid for
commercial use**. The previous dual `MIT OR Apache-2.0` grant (recorded in the now-
superseded [ADR-0012](ADR-0012.md)) is OSI-permissive and cannot express a
commercial gate: the OSD forbids discrimination against persons/groups (§5) and
fields of endeavour (§6), and a non-commercial-only license is, by definition,
neither "open source" nor "free software". A dual-license model — a public
source-available non-commercial license plus a separately negotiated commercial
license — is the standard instrument for this, but it requires owning/controlling
100% of the copyright, which in turn requires a CLA before any outside
contribution. See [relicense-advisory.md](../licensing/relicense-advisory.md) for
the full feasibility analysis, conditions, and primary-source citations.

## Decision

1. **Relicense before the first public tag** from `MIT OR Apache-2.0` to a
   **source-available, non-commercial** license. The operative text already lives
   at the repository root: the **Multiview Source-Available Non-Commercial License,
   Version 1.0** ([`LICENSE`](../../LICENSE)), with **Aperim Pty Ltd**
   (ABN 46 150 699 737) as Licensor and governing law **New South Wales,
   Australia**.

2. **Dual-license:** everything outside the free grant requires a separately
   negotiated **Commercial License** — pointer at
   [`LICENSE-COMMERCIAL.md`](../../LICENSE-COMMERCIAL.md), contact
   `licensing@aperim.com`. Free use covers genuine personal/home/non-commercial
   use plus three bespoke exceptions: First Nations Owned Broadcasters; Community
   Broadcasters under USD $1M sponsorship/yr; and Content Creators under 55,000
   subscribers summed across all channels. Business, education, government,
   productization/appliance/OEM, and streamers/creators above the threshold are
   **Commercial Use** and require the paid license.

3. **Require a CLA** (Apache-ICLA-style, augmented with an explicit
   inbound = outbound **plus the right to relicense under other terms including
   commercial/proprietary**) before accepting any outside contribution, so the
   *entire* codebase can lawfully be offered under the commercial terms. Bare DCO
   (`Signed-off-by`) is insufficient for the commercial-sale side.

4. **Stop calling the project "open source" or "free software."** Describe
   Multiview as **source-available** (optionally "source-available, free for
   non-commercial use"). Scrub "open source"/"FOSS"/"OSS" self-descriptions from
   the repo, docs, README, web SPA, and package metadata.

5. **Keep the dependency-licensing posture unchanged.** This decision changes only
   *Multiview's own* license. FFmpeg remains LGPL and the default build stays
   LGPL-clean; `gpl-codecs` remains GPL and strictly opt-in; the NDI SDK remains
   proprietary and runtime-loaded. Native Dante is NOT supported (per
   [ADR-T010](ADR-T010.md)); AES67 / ST 2110-30 is the clean, public Dante interop path
   (no SDK, no encumbrance). The `gpl-codecs`/`ndi` encumbered builds **must never ship in
   the public channel** under the non-commercial license — an encumbered-artifact
   CI split (separate, gated distribution path) follows from this ADR.

## Consequences

- Multiview is **not** OSI open source and **not** free software. SPDX tooling,
  package registries, and "open source" badges no longer apply to Multiview's own
  license; only the factual "LGPL-clean" statement about *FFmpeg linkage* remains.
- A **CLA is mandatory** before the first external PR (automated via a CLA gate).
  Until then, sole authorship means all rights are already held.
- Third-party permissive code already in the tree stays usable and redistributable
  inside the larger source-available work, but **cannot** be sold under the
  commercial license as if it were ours — its upstream terms continue to govern.
  Maintain a `THIRD-PARTY-NOTICES` manifest (generate with `cargo-about`) and scope
  the commercial license to *our* code plus a pass-through of upstream terms.
- An **encumbered-artifact CI/distribution split** is required so the
  `gpl-codecs`/`ndi` builds never reach the public source-available channel.
- A qualified software-licensing lawyer (ideally one who drafts source-available
  licenses and handles Australian trademark/media-regulation matters) must review
  the final public license, the commercial license, the CLA, and the three
  exceptions before the first public release. See
  [relicense-advisory.md](../licensing/relicense-advisory.md) — that document is
  explicitly **not** legal advice.

## Alternatives considered

- **Keep `MIT OR Apache-2.0`** (status quo, [ADR-0012](ADR-0012.md)). Rejected: it
  is OSI-permissive and structurally **cannot** express a commercial gate or the
  three free exceptions (OSD §5/§6).
- **A copyleft license (e.g. AGPL).** Rejected: copyleft still permits unrestricted
  commercial use and does not produce a paid-commercial revenue path; it is also a
  poor fit for an appliance/OEM productization tier.
- **Full copyright assignment instead of a CLA.** Rejected: maximal rights but
  highest contributor friction; an Apache-ICLA-style CLA with an explicit
  relicensing grant is sufficient to dual-license and sell.
- **Relicense after a public release.** Rejected: once a permissive version is
  public, it can be forked and continued under the original terms forever; the
  pre-public window is decisive and closing.
